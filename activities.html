--- layout: page title: Activities ---

We've lived in New York for a while now (~7 years) but still find new things to do all the time. Below is a list of some of our favorite things to do, food to eat, and drinks to drink in the city that never sleeps. If you're going to come to NY, you might as well make a weekend out of it!

Restaurants

Ruby's

219 Mulberry Street
Australian restaurant in the heart of Nolita serving one of our favorite burgers. We recommend the Bronte. Lively atmosphere, perfect for celebrity sightings - who knows, you may even run into Zachary Quinto.

Artichoke Pizza

111 Macdougal Street
Awesome pizza place in the West Village. Grab a classic New York slice or go for the decadent artichoke slice (but check with your cardiologist first).

The Taco Shop

166 West 4th Street
Small, low-key, underground taco place in the West Village. After a few tacos, head across the street to Four-Faced Liar for some brews.

Blue Water Grill

31 Union Square W
Feel like treating yourself and your special someone? Dine on the Chilean Sea Bass while listening to live jazz downstairs.

Bars

Duplex

61 Christopher Street
Don't have time for a Broadway show? Sing along at this piano bar to pop songs and showtunes.

Fat Cat

75 Christopher Street
You're never too old to play some ping pong or pool, drink cheap beer, listen to live jazz, and get your hands X'd alongside the NYU crowd.

Grisly Pear

107 Macdougal Street
Fun bar with all the fixings including karaoke in the back. Don't worry, nobody's going to remember your voice.

The Orient Express

325 West 11th Street
Tiny cocktail bar good for a strong drink at any hour.

The Spotted Pig

314 West 11th Street
Hip place good for a pig roast or a mean cocktail.

Parks

The High Line

Elevated meatpacking railroad turned park that runs above the street from 14th St to 34th St. A favorite of ours for sightseeing and people watching.

Union Square

A popular farmers market, Hare Krishnas, protests, chess players, and a dog park - what's not to love?

Washington Square Park

Welcoming you with a beautiful arch, this park is liveliest at night teeming with musicians, artists, skateboarders, and pigeon men.

Museums

The Whitney

99 Gansevoort Street
Visit this modern art museum newly relocated in the Meatpacking District. Come for the paintings and stay for the rooftop views and drinks. Head to the Standard Biergarten for a beer and pretzel afterwards.

Shopping

Intersection of Prince Street and Broadway

Every clothing store in the world has setup shop along Broadway here. Head west of Broadway for boutique shops along cobblestone streets.
